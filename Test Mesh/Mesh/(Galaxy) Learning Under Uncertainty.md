# Learning Under Uncertainty

What happens when the map is incomplete and the terrain shifts beneath your feet? This galaxy traces the transition from classical planning — where actions have known outcomes and the world holds still — to reinforcement learning, where the agent must act under uncertainty, learn from consequences, and balance the tension between exploiting what it knows and exploring what it does not.

The journey begins with a simple question about umbrellas and rain, builds through the formal machinery of Markov Decision Processes and expected utility, and arrives at agents that teach themselves to walk, manage warehouses, and navigate stochastic grid worlds. Along the way, the concepts of uncertainty, delayed consequences, reward shaping, and state design emerge not as abstract ideas but as engineering decisions that determine whether an agent thrives or flounders. The mood is one of hard-won pragmatism: the world does not cooperate, the rewards arrive late, and the agent must learn to act anyway.
