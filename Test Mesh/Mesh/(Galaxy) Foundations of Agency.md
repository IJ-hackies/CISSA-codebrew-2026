# Foundations of Agency

What does it mean for a machine to decide? This galaxy gathers the foundational ideas: the Atari agent staring at pixels, the student choosing between studying and scrolling, the rat pressing levers in search of cheese. Here you meet the three pillars of an intelligent agent — policy, value function, and model — and watch them emerge from first principles through Markov states, reward hypotheses, and the recursive elegance of the Bellman equation. Two formalisms appear side by side: the propositional certainty of STRIPS and PDDL, and the probabilistic flow of Markov reward processes. They seem like different languages, but they describe the same world — one through logic, the other through numbers — and understanding both is the price of admission to modern AI.
